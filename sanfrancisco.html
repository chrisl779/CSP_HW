San Francisco

Introduction

San Francisco resides in northern California and is the 4th most populated city in California. It is the second most densely populated city, only behind New York. San Francisco is truly one of the most beautiful cities, with a rich background. Additionally, it is the cultural, commerical, and financial heart of California.

San Francisco is home to many famous attractions. The Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, Golden Gate Park, Embarcadaro, and San Francisco Opera are all incredibly popular sites. Annually, over 1.7 million international visitors come to San Francisco. Similarly, San Francisco's scenery is simply gorgeous. Visiting San Francisco is truly an experience and should visit San Francisco at least once in their lifetime!
Come see what San Francisco's all about!




History

The name "San Francisco" translates to "Saint Francis" in Spanish, referring to Saint Francis of Assisi. First named San Francisco in 1847, the city has seen various nicknames since, such as "San Fran" or "Frisco"

Although records show that San Francisco has been inhabitated by humans for over 3000 years, much of its history has consisted of small native american tribes. In 1769, the Spanish Empire claimed California, including San Francisco.

Attractions

The Golden Gate Bridge

The Golden Gate Bridge is the most instantly recognizable bridge of all time. It spans ~1 mile over the San Francisco strait, and its distinct "international orange" color can be seen for miles in every direction.

Alcatraz Island

Alcatraz Island was once a maximum-security federal prison from 1934-1963. While in operation, Alcatraz was widely considered to be inescapable; there have been no official escapes. Alcatraz held many infamous criminals, such as Al Capone, Machine Gun Kelly, and Arthur Baker. Since 1973, Alcatraz Island has been preserved as a museum, open to all visitors.


The Embarcadaro

The Embarcadaro is the eastern waterfront of the San Francisco Port. The historically vital economic center has now become a major hotspot for tourists. Fisherman's Wharf, which includes Pier 39 and The Aquarium of the Bay, has one of the most beautiful views of San Francisco Bay. The Exploratorium is a science and technology based museum; perfect for families! Furthermore, the Embarcadaro has outstanding food options: The Waterbar, Hog Island Oyster Co, and Coqueta have fantastic tastes and views.




How to get there (from New York)

The shortest plane route from New York to San Francisco is 2,565.68 miles, which would take ~6.5 hours.

The shortest land route from New York to San Francisco is 2,902.75 miles, which would take ~43 hours of nonstop driving.

In addition, San Francisco's Pacific Standard Time (PST) is 3 hours behind New York's Eastern Standard Time (EST).
